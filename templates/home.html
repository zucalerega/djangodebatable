Welcome to Debatable!

We're so glad that you're here. But, we just want to go over the ins and outs of the site so you can have the best possible experience on debatable. Debatable is not a social media site in the conventional sense. Users mainly engage in discourse through private or group chats that can be found by navigating to the chats page in the navigation bar above. For the best experience, we suggest that you sign up by clicking on the profile icon and take the ideology quiz to accurately gauge your polotical biases. This allows us the ability to match you better to help foster useful discorse. If you're here with a school or educational oraganization, navigate to the book icon to set that up. After logging in, you can customize your profile by clicking on your icon in the navigation bar. Our mission is to provide a platform for users to engage in a civil political discorse. To moderate this policy, we encourage users to accurately rate their opponent by giving them a respect score after you finish your discussion. The weight of your rating is based on your opponents as well as your respect going into the chat. Debatable also has a community post feature where you can post any text that is allowed by the 1st ammendment. If your run into hate speech, threats, or discriminitory language of any sort in a chat or a post, please submit an anonymous report to us and we will notify you of the result. Thank you, and happy debating!

If you're looking to increase your respect, here are some quick tips!

  • Act civilized in your chats. This will help your case a lot when your opponnent goes to rate you! Attack the argument not the person.
  • Rate your opponents fairly, when your rating is similar to your opponents prior rating you gain credibility.
  • Listen to your opponents points. They will not think very highly of you if you just ignore everything that they say.
  • Back up all of your claims with facts and/or evidence. People will listen and respect you more if they know that you aren't just spouting nonsense.
  • Try to find common ground and start from there.
  • Be open to change. Going in with a growth mindset will help you see where your opponent is coming from.
